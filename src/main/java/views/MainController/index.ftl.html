General Information

What is SAT?

The SAT is a standardized test widely used for college admissions in the United States. It was first introduced in 1926, and its name and scoring have changed several times, being originally called the Scholastic Aptitude Test, then the Scholastic Assessment Test, then the SAT Reasoning Test, and now simply the SAT.

What is TOEFL?

Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL, /ˈtoʊfəl/, toh-fəl) is a standardized test of English language proficiency for non-native English language speakers wishing to enroll in US universities. The test is accepted by many English-speaking academic and professional institutions. TOEFL is one of the two major English-language tests in the world, the other being the IELTS.

Changes to the methodology this year

Changes to the methodology this year relate to the “citations per faculty” indicator. Assessed using data from Scopus, the world’s largest database of research abstracts and citations, this indicator reflects the impact and reach of each university’s research, relative to the size of its faculty. The citations per faculty indicator contributes 20% to the overall ranking results, alongside indicators which assess academic reputation (40%), employer reputation (10%), student/faculty ratio (20%), and proportions of international faculty and students (5% each).

National Scholarship Test (ORT)

National Scholarship Test (ORT) - a mandatory exam for all students who enroll in universities of Kyrgyzstan. National testing is carried out in Kyrgyzstan in 2002 by Presidential Decree. The "National Scholarship Test" administered by the Center for Educational Assessment and Teaching Methods (CEATM) in close cooperation with the Ministry of Education and Science of the Kyrgyz Republic, with the financial support of the US Agency for International Development (USAID). Since 2011, the results of Republic-wide testing became mandatory for admission not only in the form of training grants, contract and daily form of education in universities of Kyrgyzstan. The tests were developed by the independent testing organization CEATM based on the best world achievements testology. Testing is designed to identify students who are most likely to successfully continue their studies at higher educational institutions. The test is conducted in Kyrgyz and Russian (up to 2013 and in Uzbek) languages ​​and all versions of the test are equivalent in complexity. In testing applicants must demonstrate the ability to think and apply their knowledge in school.
